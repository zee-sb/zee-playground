## 1:25 — 1:55 · Clustering & themes (30 min)

Move to Section F (clustering zone).

**You facilitate:**
- Drag stickies into clusters. The team can call out "this one + that one are the same thing."
- Each cluster gets a **bold red label sticky** = the theme name. Keep theme names verb-y: "Make starters do work" beats "Improve conversation starters."
- Aim for **8–14 themes total**. If you have 30, you're not clustering hard enough. If you have 5, you're force-merging.

**When to push:**
- If two clusters feel like the same theme, ask the original authors: "Are these one theme or two?"
- If a theme is exactly "Navigator 2.0 again," surface it — that's a signal we already have the right bet, not that 2.0 absorbs everything.

**Last 5 min: dot vote.**
Each person gets 5 dots. Place them on themes (not stickies). Multiple dots on one theme allowed up to 2.

Top themes will surface as the bet candidates.

---

## 1:55 — 2:35 · Bet shaping (40 min)

Move to Section G (bet shaping). Take the top **6–8 themes** by dot count.

For each theme, fill in a Bet Card:
- **Hypothesis** ("If we ___ then ___" — must include a metric)
- Phase (T / D / R / I — often more than one)
- Metric to watch (prefer non-LLM-judge: MAU, repeat-use rate, recovery rate, branch activation)
- Confidence 1-5
- Effort S/M/L/XL
- Quarter
- Owner candidate
- What this replaces or shrinks (force the trade-off conversation)

**Split into 2-3 sub-groups** (3 people each) to fill 2-3 cards each in parallel. Time-box each group to **15 min for the first card, 10 for subsequent**. Then bring back together for read-out (5 min per card).

**Where this usually gets stuck:**
- Hypothesis is vague ("improve quality"). Force it back: *if we do X, then [metric] moves by [amount]*.
- Effort estimates are aspirational. Ask "is this 2-week-effort or 2-quarter-effort?"
- "What this replaces" is the hardest line. **Don't let any card leave the block without it filled in.** This is the moment you separate strategy from wishlist.

## 2:35 — 3:05 · Prioritization 2×2 (30 min)

Move to Section H. Drag the Bet Cards onto the Impact × Effort matrix.

**Frame it clearly:**
> "Impact here means *expected MAU lift in 2026*, not 'general goodness.' If a bet doesn't have a credible MAU story, that's information."

**Use dot color for confidence:**
- Green = we know this will move the metric
- Yellow = we think so
- Red = we'd need a spike to know

**The conversation to invite, not steer:**
- *Bottom-right (high lift, low effort) → these go first.*
- *Top-left (high lift, high effort) → these are the strategic bets — pick 1-2, not 4.*
- *Bottom-left (low lift, low effort) → low priority unless they unblock something else.*
- *Top-right (low lift, high effort) → kill unless someone makes a strong case.*

**Specific things to put pressure on:**
- Any bet in top-left should have a milestone story (Q3 / Q4 break points).
- Any "red dot" (low confidence) high-effort bet needs a spike-first sub-card.
- Be honest about what gets de-scoped from Nav 2.0 if we add new bets. It's a fixed engineering capacity.

## Failure modes to watch for (Zee's pocket card)

| If this happens | Do this |
|---|---|
| Quiet team — nothing in the buckets | Lower the bar publicly: "I'll put two bad ideas in each bucket right now to show the floor is low." Then do it. |
| Loud voice dominates discussion | Switch to silent dot-vote mid-conversation. Voting beats arguing. |
| Someone is litigating Nav 2.0 itself | "We decided that on May 6. Today is about everything else." If it persists, park it and address 1:1. |
| Bets are vague ("be better at quality") | Force a specific metric and a specific user moment. "Who, doing what, on what surface." |
| Effort estimates are uniformly "M" | Ask: "Compared to Conversation Continuity, is this bigger or smaller?" Benchmark forces honesty. |
| Time slip on clustering | Cut bet-shaping to 30 min. Don't cut commit time. |
| Two team members disagree on a bet's value | "Both of you, draft a 3-sentence version of your case. We'll come back to it next week." Don't try to resolve it in front of the room. |
| You realize mid-workshop the 4 current bets need to *all* change | That's a real outcome. Sit with it. Don't paper over. Better to leave with "we don't have alignment yet, here's what we'll do in the next 2 weeks to get there" than to fake-converge. |
